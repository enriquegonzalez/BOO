Boo a halloween ball

A night of terrifying tales, dancing, and delicious craft cocktails.

10/31/2013 7:00–11:00 p.m.

Listen to Chicago ghost stories in our galleries, and dance the fright away with DJ Nix. Drink the fright away with BOO-zy potions crafted by mad scientists Scofflaw Man and etc. Dress in your most boo-tiful costumes. Enter to win a fright courtesy of United Airlines, official airlines of the Chicago History Mausoleum. Missing out on this event would be a grave mistake. Eat drink and be scary. Haberdash, Chicago’s most popular men’s bootique.

Ghost Stories

Experience theatrical storytelling of The Devil Baby at Hull House, Resurrection Mary, The Clown on the Bank, and more adaptations of Chicago folklore by Jessica Thebus and Michele Volansky, directed by Jessica Thebus.

Jessica Thebus is an Associate Artist with Steppenwolf Theatre Company. She is currently a faculty member in the Directing Program at Northwestern and an artistic associate at The Corn Exchange in Dublin, Ireland.

music

BOO-gie down on the dancefloor with spooky sounds. Enjoy her spooky sounds tonight on our devilish dance floor.

Jena Nixon aka DJ Nix, founder of Gold Noise DJs, spins all over town from her weekly stints at The Bedford to Studio Paris and the Hubbard Inn. She spent six years DJing professionally in Buffalo, NY, before moving to Chicago.

Mixologists

Danny Shapiro is head bartender and partner at Scofflaw. When he's not behind the bar, he is often researching recipes or scouring the land for unique spirits.

Justin Cochren has been a professional mixologist for the past 8 years. He prefers working with barrel-aged spirits. Justin opened Tavernita and JParker, and also currently does brand work for Hennessy.

Paul Bastien started bartending at 18, and entered the craft beverage scene in 2010 tending the beer pub Blokes & Birds. He currently resides at DrumBar, the 18th floor cocktail bar in the Rafaello Hotel.
